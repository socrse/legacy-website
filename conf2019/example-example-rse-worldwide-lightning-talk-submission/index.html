Example RSE Worldwide lightning talk submission

Title and abstract

Research Data Management Lessons

Research data management is an important aspect of the research lifecycle. It has received a lot of attention in the past few years, due to the introduction of new European regulations (GDPR), and the growing awareness of an increasing number of research results that cannot be duplicated.

The authors are affiliated with a large, research-focused University, and their role is to assist researchers in the Department of Future History with their research data management. This is both administratively (writing or reviewing DMPs), educationally (giving lectures and tutorials) and technically (writing ICT tools to streamline research data management).

The authors would like to promote discussion about the common hurdles of research data management, such as: safely storing huge amounts of research data, finding the right metadata schemes, running sensible queries on these datasets, and visualising heaps of time series data. The authors have recently created a framework and solutions toolkit to help researchers overcome some of these hurdles, and are confident that exposure to a larger audience would stimulate discussion of appropriate solutions in this domain. Additionally, they hope to find colleagues facing similar problems so that they can share their successes and failures.

Outcomes

Greater awareness of these issues concerning research data management will be of significant use to RSEs in all environments, not just the EU. We would expect all attendees to take away useful lessons or questions.

Accessibility

I will prepare my lightning talk slides that will be uploaded to the conference app to be shared all participants before the conference. I will ensure that my slides use suitably large and legible fonts, and that I have checked my colour choices so that all figures are understandable for people who are colour-blind.

Promotion

I will upload the lightning talk slides to the conference app and website as discussed above and will promote these on twitter and on the dedicated RSE19 conference channel on the UKRSE slack to encourage attendance. I will provide an email address to allow the audience to pre-submit questions, and will get in contact with communities of Foos and Bars to let them know that I am presenting this lightning talk at RSE19.

Mentorship

This will be the first time I’ve presented in a plenary setting, and I am a little nervous of standing up and speaking in front of the whole conference. I would really like a mentor who can provide peer support while I am writing my talk and who can listen to a run-through of my lightning talk to provide useful, constructive feedback.
