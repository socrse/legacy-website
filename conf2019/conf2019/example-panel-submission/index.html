Example panel submission

Title and abstract

Understanding the impact of Foo on the community

The aim of this panel will be to discuss the impact of Foo on the wider RSE community. We will have a diverse panel of four RSEs who have come up with novel strategies for dealing with Foo, and will seed the panel discussion using the following three questions:

How has Foo impacted your organisation over the last year?
What worked, and what didn’t work, when you tackled Foo? What advice would you give to others?
How could funders and policy makers make it easier for the community to better account for Foo?

Audience

This panel will be of interest to RSEs, policy makers and funders who are either concerned about Foo, who are impacted by Foo at the moment, or who have had success or failure dealing with Foo in their organisation that they would like to share with others.

Outcomes

We will write up a blog post for the RSE website that will summarise the issues around Foo, detail current experiences of the community, and suggest a possible way forward for how the entire community can deal with Foo in the future.

Accessibility

We will prepare slides that will be uploaded to the conference app to be shared all participants before the panel. These slides will clearly detail the background on Foo and will include the three questions above. We will make use of the conference-provided microphones for all panellists and roving microphones for the audience. We will write up notes on an etherpad that can be viewed by all panel participants and audience. We will provide a contact address for less confident audience members to anonymously email questions or comments before the panel starts, and will monitor the etherpad for live questions.

Promotion

We will upload the slides including panel questions to the conference app as discussed above and will promote these on twitter and on the dedicated RSE19 conference channel on the UKRSE slack to encourage attendance and also pre-submission of questions.

Mentorship

This will be the first panel we have organised so we would request mentorship. In particular we would like feedback on the questions we are asking, and help selecting a diverse and representative panel.

Technological Requirements

We will need microphones for the panellists and chair, 2 roving microphones for the audience, wifi connection for everyone to connect to etherpad, and a projector to display our slides including questions in large print so that they are readable by all.
